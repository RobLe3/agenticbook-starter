## Part III: Challenge Protocol

When new research or a reader's correction contradicts a domain claim in the manuscript:

1. **Identify the specific claim** in the manuscript text.
2. **Locate the claim in the CFR** (Doc 13). Determine its HC entry number.
3. **Assess the tier** using Doc 10 (Canon Rules). Is this a Tier 1, 2, or 3 fact?
4. **For Tier 3:** Adjust the manuscript text locally. Note the correction and its source in the VFR (Doc 15) under the relevant V-entry.
5. **For Tier 2:** Flag the conflict. Propose a fix that preserves Tier 1 facts. Update the CFR and VFR entries. Await human approval before applying the fix.
6. **For Tier 1:** Flag to human author immediately. Do not propose a fix. Do not proceed with further chapter generation until the Tier 1 conflict is resolved.
7. **Document the correction** in the VFR (Doc 15): date, specific claim, source of correction, resolution.

> Note for agent: The challenge protocol exists because historical scholarship develops and regulations change. A procedure that was accurate at the time of writing may have been updated by a regulatory change. A historical claim may be challenged by new archival work. The protocol prevents improvised corrections that solve one problem and create three others downstream. When in doubt: flag and stop rather than fix and continue.
