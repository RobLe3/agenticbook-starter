# Location Register — [NOVEL_TITLE]

**Project:** [NOVEL_TITLE]
**Flavor:** Novel ([WORD_COUNT_TARGET] words)

> **Note for agent:** Sensory anchors are the single most important element in this register.
> A sensory anchor is one specific, non-generic detail that makes a location unmistakably itself.
> When you are about to write a scene in any of these locations, read the sensory anchor first —
> not the physical description. The physical description gives you walls and distances. The
> sensory anchor gives you the thing that makes the reader feel they have arrived.
>
> Generic location writing occurs when an agent uses the physical description to generate
> atmosphere ("the dark archive smelled of old paper") without the specific detail that
> makes this archive, this harbour, this ship different from every other archive, harbour,
> and ship in fiction. The sensory anchor prevents this. Use it every time.
