> **Note for agent:** This glossary is structured in three categories because different terms
> activate in different scene types. Marine Archaeology terms appear in [PROTAGONIST_NAME]'s procedural
> scenes (dive preparation, lab work, documentation). Colonial West African History terms
> appear in [SECONDARY_POV_NAME]'s chapters and in the research scenes where [PROTAGONIST_NAME] and [SUPPORTING_CHARACTER_NAME] unpack
> what the diplomatic mission actually was. Narrative Craft Terms are the structural vocabulary
> you need to understand how this specific novel is built — dual POV, free indirect discourse,
> the interleaving logic. Before writing a dive scene, read Category 1. Before writing any
> Timeline B chapter, read Category 2. Read Category 3 before starting any chapter.
>
> A three-category structure prevents the most common failure mode in domain-specific
> fiction: the agent reaches for a technical term in the wrong scene type, over-explains it,
> or uses it decoratively rather than functionally. Each term here includes a Usage Note
> that tells you exactly how the term appears in *this* novel — not in textbooks.
