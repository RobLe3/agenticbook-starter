# Research Foundation — [NOVEL_TITLE]

**Project:** [NOVEL_TITLE]
**Flavor:** Novel ([WORD_COUNT_TARGET] words)

> **Note for agent:** Fiction needs a source inventory for the same reason non-fiction does:
> a hallucinated historical claim breaks reader trust faster than any prose problem.
> In literary fiction with domain-specific content (marine archaeology, colonial history,
> period chemistry), a single wrong detail activates reader scepticism about everything
> around it. A reader who knows that the Fante Confederation's founding date is 1868 and
> finds it written as 1866 in Chapter 5 will spend the rest of the novel checking other facts
> rather than inhabiting the story. This document prevents that.
>
> The structure below separates claims into three tiers:
> 1. **Known-reliable:** Verifiable historical or scientific fact. Generate with confidence.
>    If the novel contradicts these, the novel is wrong and must be corrected.
> 2. **Plausible:** Claims consistent with the historical record but not individually
>    documented — plausible given what is known. Generate with care; flag for verification.
> 3. **Needs verification before publication:** Claims that require specialist confirmation.
>    These may be used in draft generation; they must be checked before final manuscript.
>
> Do not treat "plausible" as "known-reliable." The verification tier exists precisely
> because the two feel identical when generating prose.
