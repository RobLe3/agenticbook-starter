# Doc 02: Target Readers

> **Example world model doc for *Agentic Book Writing: The Smart Way* — Chapter 5.**
> For Short flavor, 2–3 reader profiles is enough. More creates contradictory constraints
> that make the manuscript harder to write.
> Your agent uses these profiles when making voice, vocabulary, and example decisions.
> *Book Lab → Lab 5 (Chapter 5). If you completed Lab 5, compare your reader profiles to these — look for: profile specificity, goals vs. fears separation, 2–3 profile count discipline.*

---

## Primary Reader: The Complete Beginner

> **The primary reader is the person you are writing FOR.** Every word should serve them.
> The secondary reader is a bonus — do not compromise the primary reader to please them.

**Profile:** "Sam"
**Age range:** 25–45
**Background:** Has a smartphone with a decent camera. Interested in photography generally. Saw a stunning Milky Way photo on social media and thought: *I want to do that*. Has no technical photography knowledge — doesn't know what ISO stands for, has never used manual mode.

**Their situation:**
- Lives in a mid-sized city or suburb (moderate light pollution)
- Has a budget of $0–$300 for additional equipment
- Can drive 1–2 hours to reach darker skies
- Has time for one to two "shooting nights" per month

**What they want:** A concrete first win within the first weekend. Then progressive improvement they can see in their own photos.

**What blocks them:**
- Gear intimidation ("I don't have a DSLR")
- Jargon ("What's a 500 rule? What's dark adaptation?")
- Location ("Where do I even go?")
- Weather ("How do I know if tonight is a good night?")

**Success looks like:** After reading Chapter 1 and trying the smartphone lab, Sam has a real photograph of star trails from their backyard. It's not perfect. But it's real, and it's theirs. They keep reading.

> **Voice implication:** Write to Sam. Use "you." Define every technical term on first use.
> Never assume prior knowledge. Celebrate small wins. The tone is encouraging, not
> condescending — Sam is smart; they just haven't learned this yet.

## Secondary Reader: The Enthusiast Upgrader

**Profile:** "Morgan"
**Age range:** 30–55
**Background:** Has an entry-level DSLR or mirrorless camera. Has taken photos outdoors before — landscapes, travel, family events. Knows what aperture and shutter speed are but has never used manual mode for low-light photography. Has tried night photos once or twice and been disappointed with the results.

**Their situation:**
- Already owns suitable equipment (camera + kit lens)
- Familiar with basic photography vocabulary
- Can identify a few constellations, knows roughly where the Milky Way rises in summer
- Frustrated that their results don't match what they see in tutorials

**What they want:** To know what they're doing wrong, and to fix it systematically.

**What blocks them:**
- Unknown gaps ("I thought I had the right settings but my photos are blurry")
- Workflow uncertainty ("When do I process in Lightroom vs. in-camera?")
- Location strategy ("I found a dark spot but the photos still look hazy")

**Success looks like:** Morgan applies the technique from Chapter 4 (DSLR manual settings) and produces a sharper, brighter result than their previous attempts. They understand *why* the change worked.

> **Voice implication:** Morgan can tolerate technical vocabulary without definition — but
> verify in Doc 04 that the term is in the glossary before using it without definition.
> Morgan reads faster than Sam; prose can be slightly denser in technical sections.
> When writing for both readers simultaneously, write for Sam — Morgan will follow.

## Reader NOT Served by This Book

> **Defining who this is NOT for** is as important as defining who it IS for.
> It keeps the scope guardrails from Doc 01 active during generation.

This book does not serve:
- Experienced photographers who already shoot in manual mode and want advanced post-processing
- Telescope owners who want to photograph deep-sky objects
- Readers who want a comprehensive astronomy education alongside the photography
- Professionals or semi-professionals looking for gear upgrade advice

If an agent-generated section is drifting toward this audience, it is out of scope. Redirect to the primary reader profile.
