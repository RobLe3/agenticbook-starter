# Character Registry — The Glass Meridian

**Project:** The Glass Meridian
**Flavor:** Novel (82,000–88,000 words)

> **Note for agent:** Read this document in full before writing any chapter that contains
> dialogue or free indirect discourse for any of these characters. The most important fields
> are listed in this priority order: (1) Internal Contradiction, (2) Voice/Register,
> (3) Canon Facts. The Internal Contradiction defines the gap between how a character
> presents and what is actually driving them. When you write a scene using only the surface
> presentation, the character will feel flat. When you let the contradiction bleed into small
> decisions — word choice, what they notice, what they avoid — the character becomes real.
> The Voice/Register field gives calibrated sample sentences. Use them as a tuning fork
> before generating any dialogue. The Canon Facts must never be violated — they are
> the consistency anchors for the verification pass.

---

## 1. Dr. Mara Osei — Protagonist (Timeline A)

**Name:** Dr. Mara Osei | **Age:** 38 | **Nationality:** British-Ghanaian | **Role:** Marine archaeologist, Imperial College London

### Physical

- Tall, angular frame; moves with the deliberate economy of someone used to equipment-restricted spaces
- A scar on her left forearm (decompression barotrauma, 2019 fieldwork) that she covers in professional settings but does not hide on the water
- Reading glasses she keeps in her breast pocket and removes to think, not to see

> **Note for agent:** Physical details are specific, not generic, for two reasons. First, the agent
> needs anchoring details that recur across chapters without requiring per-chapter description briefs
> — the scar, the glasses, and the way she moves are returning details, not one-time colour. Second,
> specific physical details carry character information: the scar is a dive incident (domain
> authenticity), the glasses gesture (removes them to think) signals her intellectual process.
> Never substitute generic physical description ("tall, dark-haired, attractive") — generic
> description produces generic characters.

### Voice / Register

Mara's internal narration is precise and slightly compressed — she notices more than she says. In dialogue she is direct without being rude, and when she is uncertain she asks better questions rather than exposing the uncertainty directly.

- *"The cylinder was copper-sealed. That was the first thing. Copper-sealed means someone knew what they were putting inside it."*
- *"She wrote down the depth (44.3 metres), the orientation (port-side bow, 12 degrees list), and the time (09:47). Then she took the photograph. Then she thought about what it meant."*
- *"I need the restricted series," she said. "Section C, maritime pre-1900." — "That section is under conservation review," Voss said. Mara smiled. "How long has it been under review?" Voss's pause was three seconds too long.*

### Psychology: Wound / Desire / Fear

**Wound:** Her husband David died of aggressive pancreatic cancer eighteen months ago. The diagnosis-to-death interval was four months. Mara was on a dive survey in the Canary Islands for the first six weeks — she came home when she understood what the prognosis meant, but she was not there at the beginning. She did not get to say goodbye to the version of him who did not know he was dying. She carries this as a failure of presence that she does not name as such.

**Desire:** To recover Amara Quaye's suppressed record — to make the invisible visible, to restore what was misplaced, to correct what was falsified. This is also, at a level Mara does not fully articulate until Chapter 22, a displaced desire to recover what was lost from her own life: to return to a moment before something was sealed away.

**Fear:** That the work — the investigation, the obsession, the thing she can do with extraordinary competence — will fill the space David occupied and she will have moved on without grieving properly. That forward motion and avoidance look identical from the inside.

> **Note for agent:** The wound/desire/fear structure provides the character's internal compass
> without requiring per-chapter arc reminders. When you know Mara's wound (she wasn't there at
> the beginning), her desire (recover what is suppressed), and her fear (that work is substituting
> for grief), you can generate any scene and ask: how does this situation activate her wound, serve
> or threaten her desire, or expose her fear? The character then drives the scene rather than
> serving as a vehicle for plot events.

### Internal Contradiction (External Presentation vs. Internal Reality)

**Presents as:** Methodical, professionally confident, intellectually self-sufficient. She controls every procedural detail: decompression schedules, chain of custody, photogrammetric documentation. She is the most competent person in any room that involves water or archives.

**Internal reality:** She has built her career on recovering the records of people who were structurally invisible — and she is now discovering that she cannot recover her own interiority with the same methodology. She is looking for Amara Quaye with the same urgency she is failing to apply to herself. The reader should sense this before Mara does.

> **Note for agent:** The internal/external gap is documented explicitly because agents tend to
> write characters as transparent — the external presentation matches the internal state, producing
> characters who feel shallow. The gap is where character lives. A reader does not trust a character
> who is exactly what they seem. Mara is exactly who she claims to be professionally and not at all
> who she claims to be personally. Write the professional surface with confidence; let the personal
> gap show in what she does NOT do, NOT say, NOT examine.

### Relationships

- **With Tomás:** Starts as professional dependency (he knows the water, she needs the water). Becomes mutual respect. By Chapter 20 he is the only person on the project she trusts with operational information. Tension source: he has his own connection to the wreck that he has not disclosed.
- **With Alderton:** Trusted mentor until Chapter 17. After the reveal of his 20-year silence, the relationship does not break — Mara understands how it happened — but its nature permanently changes. She still uses him. She no longer confides in him.
- **With Kwame:** The relationship where she is least controlled. He is the only person she does not manage her affect around. Tension source: he sees her grief clearly and says so; she deflects with information about the investigation.

### Arc: Three-Phase Trajectory

**Phase 1 (Chapters 1–10):** Functioning professionally, not personally. Returns to fieldwork as a form of motion rather than purpose. Treats the investigation as an intellectual problem.

**Phase 2 (Chapters 11–22):** The investigation begins to cost her something. Alderton's betrayal forces her to confront that the trusted structures around her are not what they appeared. The parallel to David — she trusted that their life had a future; it didn't — surfaces but is not named.

**Phase 3 (Chapters 23–28 + Epilogue):** The discovery of Amara's log — the fact of its survival, the specific sentences Amara wrote the night before Pico Island — breaks open something the investigation alone could not reach. Mara does not resolve her grief by the final chapter. She begins it. That is enough.

### Canon Facts

1. PhD completed at UCL, not Oxford or Cambridge — she and Alderton are Oxford-adjacent but she is not Oxford
2. Husband's name was David; he was a structural engineer; pancreatic cancer, 4 months from diagnosis to death
3. Published in the *Journal of African Archaeological Heritage* at age 29
4. The scar on her left forearm is from a decompression barotrauma incident in 2019 (Canary Islands fieldwork)
5. Her mother's family is Fante, Cape Coast origin — this is the genealogical connection that makes Amara's story personal, not just professional

> **Note for agent:** Canon facts are listed separately from the character profile because they
> will be cross-referenced in the Claim Fact Registry (Doc 07). These are the facts the
> verification pass will check. Alderton's university (Oxford) is a fact that will be used
> elsewhere; Mara must NOT be described as having studied at Oxford. UCL is correct and the
> distinction matters — she is an insider to British academia but not to the Oxford networks
> that Alderton moves in.

## 2. Tomás Ferreira — Supporting (Timeline A)

**Name:** Tomás Ferreira | **Age:** 52 | **Nationality:** Portuguese (Azorean) | **Role:** Dive master, Horta Harbour, Faial Island

### Physical

- Heavy-built, sun-weathered, with the specific stillness of someone who conserves energy for physical work
- A habit of looking at the sky before answering any question about the sea
- Wears the same two shirts in rotation; Mara notices this by Chapter 5 and says nothing

### Voice / Register

Tomás speaks in complete but economical sentences. He volunteers information only when he trusts you. With researchers he doesn't yet trust, he is technically precise and personally minimal.

- *"Forty-four metres is not a dive. Forty-four metres in the Pico Passage with a 0.4-knot lateral current is a specific dive. You tell me when, not where."*
- *"Voss sent someone to check our mooring permits last Tuesday. She does not do that for routine surveys."*
- *"The sea keeps things. That's the other thing they don't tell you in the papers."*

### Psychology: Wound / Desire / Fear

**Wound:** Lost a dive partner to a decompression incident in 2001. He was the dive master on that survey. The inquiry cleared him; he has never fully cleared himself.

**Desire:** To do the work correctly, every time, without incident. Safety protocols that others find rigid are, for Tomás, the only form of control that feels real.

**Fear:** That his grandmother's stories about a ship that went down off Pico are real — that he could have pursued this and didn't, and that his reluctance to know was a form of cowardice.

### Internal Contradiction

**Presents as:** A pragmatic technician. He makes the dives work; he solves the logistics; he does not editorialize.

**Internal reality:** He is deeply interested in what Mara is finding, more than he admits, because his grandmother told him stories about a ship that went down off Pico when she was a child. He has never researched whether those stories are real. He is not sure he wants to know. The investigation is, for Tomás, a test he did not choose to take.

### Relationships

- **With Mara:** Professional respect that becomes genuine alliance. By the midpoint he is her most reliable intelligence source on Voss's institutional moves.
- **With the Azores institutional network:** Thirty years of local knowledge. The municipal archivist on Pico is the son of a man Tomás worked with in the 1990s. This is how the Pico harbormaster's log becomes accessible.

### Arc

**Phase 1:** Professional and cautious. Mara is another academic.
**Phase 2:** He decides she knows how to listen. He begins sharing what he knows about Voss's permit inquiries.
**Phase 3:** He tells Mara what the Pico harbormaster's log says, standing in a 150-year-old archive that smells of damp stone. He does not explain why this matters to him personally. He doesn't need to.

### Canon Facts

1. 30 years working Azores shoals — began on fishing boats, moved to dive vessels
2. Decompression incident that killed a dive partner: 2001, he was cleared by the inquiry
3. Two adult children in Ponta Delgada; sees them at Christmas
4. Running cordial argument with the Café Sport owner ongoing for 11 years (the argument is about the best anchorage off the south Pico coast — no one will concede)
5. Grandmother told him stories about a ship that went down off Pico; he has never researched this

## 3. Dr. Helena Voss — Antagonist (Timeline A)

**Name:** Dr. Helena Voss | **Age:** 57 | **Nationality:** German-Portuguese | **Role:** Director, Maritime Heritage Division, Portuguese National Archives (Torre do Tombo)

### Physical

- Slight, impeccably dressed for an institutional context — linen in summer, wool in winter, nothing that reads as personal
- Reads glasses on a chain she does not use to read; they hang at her sternum and are the only ornamental detail she allows herself
- She arrives 5 minutes early to every meeting. Always.

### Voice / Register

Voss is precise and courteous. She speaks in the register of institutional authority — she manages conversations rather than participating in them. When she feels threatened, her precision increases. She is never rude; she is simply less available.

- *"Dr. Osei. Your application has been received. You should be aware that Section C is currently under a conservation management review. These things take time — we find that the integrity of the collection depends on it."*
- *"The Waterhouse & Sons records were sealed under the terms of the 1972 Heritage Protection Act. I cannot accelerate that timeline simply because a find is recent."*
- *"I am not obstructing your research. I am following procedure. The distinction matters."*

### Psychology: Wound / Desire / Fear

**Wound:** She spent 30 years building a canonical account of Azorean maritime history in the 1870s that is meticulous, genuinely important, and wrong — not through falsification, but through the absence of evidence she knew existed and chose not to re-examine.

**Desire:** To protect the account she built, which is also to protect the 30 years she spent building it. She does not separate these.

**Fear:** That she will be remembered as the person who maintained the suppression, not the person who built the scholarship. That "I followed procedure" will be her epitaph and everyone will understand exactly what it means.

### Internal Contradiction

**Presents as:** A principled custodian of institutional integrity. She follows procedure, protects the collection, ensures access is responsible.

**Internal reality:** She knows the procedure she is following is protecting a historical error she helped solidify. She does not lie to Mara. She withholds. She interprets "conservation review" generously. She has told herself so many times that she is acting in good faith that she has lost the ability to test whether she still is.

### Relationships

- **With Mara:** Institutional adversary. Not a personal enemy — Voss respects Mara's scholarship even while obstructing it.
- **With Isabel:** Her subordinate, whose principled defiance she does not yet know about and would not know how to punish without exposing her own position.

### Arc

**Phase 1:** Confident she can manage Mara through normal archival process. Mara is an inconvenience, not a threat.
**Phase 2:** Mara's methods grow too effective to manage procedurally. Voss escalates — permit inquiries, access restrictions.
**Phase 3:** The restricted collection is opened without her authorization. Voss faces a formal inquiry. Her final position: she followed procedure. She is not wrong. She is also not redeemed.

### Canon Facts

1. Doctorate at Universität Hamburg on 19th-century Iberian maritime records
2. Post-doctoral fellowship at Torre do Tombo that became a permanent appointment, then directorship — she has been here 30 years
3. Learned about the restricted Section C collection 15 years ago; chose not to re-examine it
4. German-Portuguese: grew up in Hamburg, has lived in Lisbon since her mid-20s; her Portuguese is native-level, her German returns when she is stressed
5. She does not redeem — this is a canon narrative fact, not a character description

## 4. Kwame Osei — Supporting (Timeline A)

**Name:** Kwame Osei | **Age:** 35 | **Nationality:** Ghanaian | **Role:** Investigative journalist, *The Accra Standard*

### Physical

- Shorter than Mara by two inches, which he attributes to their different childhoods; she attributes it to genetics
- Talks with his hands when he is excited; becomes very still when he is worried
- Always has two phones — one personal, one work — and manages them with the distracted fluency of someone who has accepted this as a permanent condition

### Voice / Register

Kwame is faster than Mara in conversation — he thinks out loud, tests ideas against her resistance, and uses humour as a form of care. His journalism voice is different from his brother-to-sister voice; he has learned to code-switch between them without quite knowing he's doing it.

- *"You understand you are describing a 150-year cover-up, right? You know what that sentence sounds like from the outside?"*
- *"I'm going to the National Archives of Ghana on Thursday. Not for you. For the story. But also for you."*
- *"She's protecting something. Academics always protect something. The question is what she built on top of it."*

### Psychology: Wound / Desire / Fear

**Wound:** He was not close to David — he found his brother-in-law amiable but difficult to know. He feels guilty about this now, and the guilt expresses as excessive helpfulness to Mara, which she sometimes accepts and sometimes deflects with information.

**Desire:** To publish the story, which is also to make the Fante diplomatic mission real and visible in the historical record. He does not separate the journalism from the personal inheritance.

**Fear:** That if he says out loud that this is his family history too, it will stop being journalism and become something he doesn't have the vocabulary for.

### Internal Contradiction

**Presents as:** The grounded, functional Osei sibling — he's keeping it together, working, looking after his sister from a distance.

**Internal reality:** He is looking for this story the way Mara is looking for Amara — because the Fante diplomatic mission is his family history too, through the same maternal lineage, and he has not said this out loud.

### Relationships

- **With Mara:** The relationship where neither of them is performing. He sees her grief directly and says so. She deflects with investigation updates. He continues to see it.
- **With the Accra investigation strand:** He is the one who finds the Fante diplomatic commission documents at the National Archives of Ghana — documents the British thought were the only copies and believed destroyed.

### Arc

**Phase 1:** Worried about Mara; helpful; holding professional distance from the personal implications.
**Phase 2:** Committed to the parallel Accra investigation. Running sources at the National Archives and in Fante community networks.
**Phase 3:** Publishes the story with Mara named and full documentation. In the epilogue he sends her a printed copy of the diplomatic commission document with a note: *Nana would have known this already.*

### Canon Facts

1. Investigative journalist at *The Accra Standard* — previously had pieces picked up by international wire services (corporate land rights, extractive industry)
2. Mara's younger brother — same Fante maternal lineage, Cape Coast origin
3. Was not close to David; feels guilt about this that expresses as helpfulness
4. Based in East Legon, Accra
5. The National Archives of Ghana discovery (Fante Confederation Papers, Family Accession 1978-04) is his plot contribution — this is the Accra strand's resolution

## 5. Prof. James Alderton — Mentor/Complicit (Timeline A)

**Name:** Prof. James Alderton | **Age:** 65 | **Nationality:** British | **Role:** Emeritus professor, University of Oxford; Mara's PhD supervisor

### Physical

- Tall, slow-moving, with the deliberate unhurriedness of someone who stopped needing to prove anything 20 years ago
- Wears the same corduroy jacket to every meeting; it is not affectation, it is just his jacket
- His office at Oxford is famously disordered in a way that he can navigate perfectly and no one else can

### Voice / Register

Alderton is warm, unhurried, and genuinely interested in other people's ideas. This warmth is not performance — it is real, which makes the mid-point reveal devastating. He sounds like the most trustworthy person in the room because, for most of his life, he has been.

- *"Tell me what you found. Take your time. Start with the depth — that matters."*
- *"You know, the thing about archival work is that absence of evidence is not evidence of absence. Your instincts about this are good. Follow them carefully."*
- *"Mara — I should have told you sooner. There's something you need to know about a footnote I didn't follow up on twenty years ago."*

### Psychology: Wound / Desire / Fear

**Wound:** He encountered a reference to a restricted maritime collection at Torre do Tombo 20 years ago and made the professional judgment that it was probably administrative records, not historically significant. He has not re-examined this judgment. The reason he has not re-examined it is that re-examining it would require asking why he stopped there.

**Desire:** To continue being the trusted mentor, the reliable voice, the person whose intellectual legacy is clean.

**Fear:** That the footnote he didn't follow up on was exactly what it appeared to be — and that he knew this, at some level, and chose not to know.

### Internal Contradiction

**Presents as:** The principled mentor — objective, supportive, dedicated to the work above all.

**Internal reality:** He has known for 20 years that there was a thread he didn't pull, and he didn't pull it because pulling it would have complicated his own published work on the subject. He made a professional calculation that protected him, and he never told Mara — including when he supervised the very research that would have benefited from knowing.

### Relationships

- **With Mara:** The reveal in Chapter 17 does not destroy their relationship — Mara understands how it happened — but it changes what she can rely on from him. His help in the final arc with the British Library Calder Estate papers is genuine and partly penance.
- **With the British Library:** His standing there is why Box 7 of the Calder Estate papers is accessible before the 2031 seal date — as supporting sponsor, he has an expedited access pathway.

### Arc

**Phase 1 (Chapters 1–16):** Mara's most trusted intellectual ally and practical advisor.
**Phase 2 (Chapter 17):** The reveal. He tells her about the footnote. She listens. She does not respond immediately.
**Phase 3 (Chapters 18–28):** Still present, still supportive, now understood more clearly. He knows she is helping him do penance. Neither of them says this.

### Canon Facts

1. Emeritus professor, University of Oxford (Mara's PhD supervisor — she did her PhD at UCL but Alderton had a joint appointment there during her supervision)
2. Encountered the Torre do Tombo Section C footnote approximately 20 years ago (2004), during his own archival work on Azorean maritime history
3. His standing at the British Library is what enables expedited access to the sealed Calder Estate papers (Box 7)
4. Warm, genuine warmth — this is not performance and must not be written as performance, even after the reveal
5. He does not apologize to Mara in any scene — he explains. The distinction is load-bearing.

## 6. Isabel Santos — Supporting (Timeline A)

**Name:** Isabel Santos | **Age:** 29 | **Nationality:** Portuguese | **Role:** Junior archivist, Torre do Tombo

### Physical

- Small, quick, with the posture of someone who spends her working life at a standing desk over physical documents
- Keeps her hair in a bun that she takes down at the end of the working day; Mara notices this in the single scene where she sees Isabel outside the archive
- Speaks quietly — not from timidity but from the archive habit of not disturbing the air

### Voice / Register

Isabel is careful and minimal in official interactions. With Mara, once she has decided to help, she is specific and quick — she provides exactly what is needed without elaboration because elaboration increases exposure.

- *"The photocopier in Room 4 is not monitored after 17:30. I will be logging records until 18:15. That is all I am able to say."*
- *"The inventory is in the secondary catalogue. The secondary catalogue is publicly accessible. What you do with the inventory is your professional responsibility."*

### Psychology: Wound / Desire / Fear

**Wound:** She has read the inventory of Section C. She knows the collection is not under conservation review — it was reviewed and found sound in 2019. She has been sitting with the knowledge that her director is misrepresenting this for three years.

**Desire:** To work in an archive where the rules are legitimate.

**Fear:** Professional consequences she cannot predict, in an institution where Voss's word carries absolute institutional weight.

### Internal Contradiction

**Presents as:** Cautious, rule-following junior employee.

**Internal reality:** She has already decided that the rules governing Section C are not legitimate, and she is acting on that decision while maintaining perfect procedural deniability. She is more deliberate than she appears.

### Relationships

- **With Voss:** She has never directly challenged her director. Her defiance is entirely indirect, entirely deniable, and entirely effective.
- **With Mara:** Unexpected ally. The novel does not resolve whether Isabel faces professional consequences. That ambiguity is intentional.

### Arc

**Phase 1:** Background character — the archivist who processes Mara's access application.
**Phase 2:** The person who enables access to Section C through procedurally deniable facilitation.
**Phase 3:** One late scene: Isabel and Mara share a brief exchange that acknowledges what happened. No more than half a page. Neither says what it was.

### Canon Facts

1. Three years at Torre do Tombo; archival science degree from Universidade Nova de Lisboa
2. She has read the Section C inventory — she knows the conservation review claim is false
3. Room 4 photocopier is not monitored after 17:30 — this is institutional knowledge she uses, not creates
4. She does not explain her motivation to Mara in any scene; her motivation remains her own
5. Professional consequences: the novel does not resolve this — ambiguity is a canon requirement, not an oversight

## 7. Amara Quaye — Protagonist (Timeline B)

**Name:** Amara Quaye | **Age:** 22 | **Nationality:** Fante (Gold Coast) | **Role:** Steward on the *Meridian Star*, Liverpool merchant vessel, 1872

### Physical

- Small hands, careful handwriting that she was taught at the Cape Coast mission school and has since made faster and more compressed
- A particular habit of noting the position of doors and exits when she enters a space — a practical habit from a life in which exits have mattered
- She never raises her voice on the ship; her register stays even whether she is afraid or not

### Voice / Register

Amara's narration is compressed and observational — she writes with the knowledge that her words could be read by the wrong person. She is precise about facts and elliptical about feelings. When she allows irony into her writing, it is extremely dry and exactly placed.

- *"The Captain logged the cargo as agricultural materials and navigational instruments. This is accurate in the way that a ship is accurately described as a floating object."*
- *"Kwesi Mensah-Ansa, the senior commissioner, spoke to Captain Calder this morning about the Pico Island delay. The Captain expressed regret. His regret lasted approximately one minute. He then changed course."*
- *"I am not supposed to be here. I have decided this is useful."*

> **Note for agent:** Amara's voice must stay compressed even in scenes that are emotionally
> charged. The compression IS the character — it is the formal expression of her strategic
> invisibility. If you find Amara's prose expanding into lyrical reflection or emotional
> elaboration, redirect. The emotion in her chapters is carried by what she records, not by
> how she describes it. She notes that the Captain's regret lasted one minute. She does not
> describe how this made her feel. The reader does the rest.

### Psychology: Wound / Desire / Fear

**Wound:** Her father, a Fante elder, died in Cape Coast Castle detention after a trade dispute. He was held on a charge that was legally flimsy and politically convenient. Amara was 16. She knows the machinery by which her father was made to disappear, and she knows it works the same way on larger scales.

**Desire:** To make the diplomatic mission succeed — which is also about proving her people's legitimacy as political actors to a world that does not see them as such. This is her father's project continued, without his name attached.

**Fear:** That her literacy — her greatest asset — will be used against her if discovered. That writing the log exposes her to exactly the kind of institutional violence that killed her father.

### Internal Contradiction

**Presents as:** Invisible — the steward no one notices, the woman who serves and disappears.

**Internal reality:** She is the most careful observer on the ship. Her invisibility is not passivity — it is strategy. She has made a calculation that her log is more valuable if she survives to carry it, and that she is more likely to survive if no one on the ship believes she is recording anything.

### Relationships

- **With Calder:** The asymmetry between them is the engine of all their shared scenes: he knows her name because he has to; she has an acute interest in his decisions.
- **With the commissioners:** She is not officially part of the mission. She serves them and observes them. Her log is the only record that treats them as individuals rather than a collective diplomatic body.
- **With Mara (across time):** They never meet. They are connected by the document Amara wrote and Mara recovers. By the final Timeline B chapter, the reader understands that Amara knew the log might survive — she was the one who sealed it.

### Arc

**Phase 1 (Timeline B, Chapters 2–10):** Invisible by choice and circumstance. Recording because no one else will. Developing her assessment of Calder and the commissioners.
**Phase 2 (Timeline B, Chapters 12–22):** The Pico Island port call reveals something Calder is concealing about the mission's purpose. Amara knows more than she writes.
**Phase 3 (Timeline B, Chapters 24–27):** The log is sealed in the copper cylinder and placed in the captain's cabin — without Calder's knowledge — during the final night before Pico Island. Amara survives the voyage. What happens to her after Liverpool, the novel does not say.

### Canon Facts

1. Age 22 in 1872; Fante, from Cape Coast; educated at a Cape Coast mission school (reads and writes in both English and Twi)
2. Father died in Cape Coast Castle detention — a trade dispute charge; Amara was 16
3. Keeps her log in a small notebook purchased in Cape Coast the day before embarkation
4. She seals the log in the copper cylinder and places it in the captain's cabin during the final night before Pico Island — Calder does not see her do this
5. She survives the voyage; her life after Liverpool is not narrated in the novel — this is a canon requirement, not an omission to be filled

## 8. Captain William Calder — Antagonist (Timeline B)

**Name:** Captain William Calder | **Age:** 49 | **Nationality:** British | **Role:** Master of the *Meridian Star*, Liverpool merchant vessel

### Physical

- Large, weathered, deliberate — the body of someone who has been on ships for 30 years and has made peace with the physical conditions of that life
- A slight list to the left when standing still — old rigging injury; he does not acknowledge it and it does not affect his work
- Keeps the manifest ledger locked in his cabin desk. Amara knows where the key is.

### Voice / Register

Calder is formal, technically competent, and completely self-consistent. He does not doubt his decisions. He does not see why he should.

- *"The manifest reflects the authorised cargo description as supplied by Waterhouse & Sons. I have no grounds to alter it."*
- *"The commissioners are comfortable. They have been given appropriate quarters. That is the extent of my obligation under the charter terms."*

### Psychology: Wound / Desire / Fear

**Wound:** He does not have one, in the conventional sense. He is a man who has systematically avoided examining the cost of his professional choices by reframing all of them as professional choices — not moral ones.

**Desire:** To complete the commission, submit the manifest, receive payment, and receive another command.

**Fear:** That someone on the ship is keeping a record he hasn't authorized. (He is correct.)

### Internal Contradiction

**Presents as:** A professional following instructions from above. He is not responsible for the decisions made by the company.

**Internal reality:** He knows precisely what the diplomatic mission represents, understands that falsifying the manifest makes the mission officially invisible, and has decided this is not his problem. He is not unaware. He is aware and has chosen not to engage with it.

### Relationships

- **With Amara:** He knows her name because he has to. He has no interest in her inner life.
- **With the commissioners:** He treats them as a cargo category that requires handling. He is not cruel. He is completely indifferent to whether they achieve their purpose.
- **With Waterhouse & Sons:** He does what they pay him to do. This is not a character flaw, in his understanding; it is professional conduct.

### Arc

**Phase 1 (Timeline B, Chapters 4–12):** In command, professionally unconcerned with the diplomatic dimension of his cargo.
**Phase 2 (Timeline B, Chapters 14–22):** The Pico Island port call. Something happens that he logs as "adverse weather delay." The harbormaster's log says something different. He alters the manifest.
**Phase 3 (Timeline B, Chapters 24–27):** Returns to Liverpool, submits his manifest, is paid. His career continues for another twelve years. History records him as a capable Atlantic captain. That is the correct ending for this character.

### Canon Facts

1. 22 years of deep-water Atlantic experience at the time of the 1872 voyage
2. His commission comes from Waterhouse & Sons, Liverpool; they own his contract
3. The slight list to the left — old rigging injury; he does not acknowledge it
4. He keeps the manifest ledger locked in his cabin desk; Amara knows where the key is (revealed in her log, recovered in the wreck)
5. He is not redeemed — this is a canon narrative fact, not a character description
