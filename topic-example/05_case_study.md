## Project Evidence

### Bake Log

Maintained a structured bake log from Year 2 onward (Year 1 was informal). Log entries include:
- Date, ambient temperature, flour type, hydration percentage
- Bulk fermentation start/end times and volume measurements
- Shaping notes, cold retard duration, baking temperature and times
- Finished crumb assessment (open/tight, texture, flavor notes)
- What-went-wrong diagnosis

**Total logged bakes at book draft date:** 847 bakes over 9 years.
**Notable log findings:**
- Temperature is the dominant variable in 73% of inconsistency cases documented
- Overfermentation caused 41% of flat/dense loaf failures
- Underfermentation caused 31% of flat/dense loaf failures
- All other causes combined: 28%

### Workshop Data

**Common failure modes across 40 workshops (cumulative):**

| Failure Mode | Frequency | Root Cause (most common) |
|---|---|---|
| Flat loaf | 38% | Overfermentation (too warm kitchen) |
| Dense crumb | 27% | Underfermentation + premature shaping |
| No oven spring | 18% | Overproofing during final proof |
| Too sour | 9% | Acetic acid overproduction (cold + long ferment) |
| Gummy crumb | 5% | Cut while hot; insufficient bake time |
| Spread, no height | 3% | Weak gluten + high hydration |

**Workshop insight:** The single most common question asked by students after their
first failed bake: "Was my starter bad?" Answer in 85% of cases: no. The starter
was fine; the problem was process timing.

## Methodology Evolution

### Year 1–2: Recipe Following Phase

Baked from published recipes exactly as written. Produced inconsistent results.
Primary frustration: recipes specified time (e.g., "bulk ferment 4 hours") rather
than readiness indicators. Rooms are not 72°F in all climates at all times.

### Year 3–4: Systematic Measurement Phase

Added kitchen thermometer, measured water temperature before mixing, began recording
bulk ferment start and end volumes rather than relying on time alone. Consistency
improved dramatically — not because technique improved, but because the process
became legible.

**Key insight from this phase:** The recipe never failed. The environment did.

### Year 5–7: Troubleshooting Framework Phase

Started working backward from observed loaf failures to process causes. Developed
a symptom-to-cause diagnosis table. Began teaching this framework in workshops.

**Surprising finding:** Students who received the diagnostic framework before
attempting their first bake had a 40% higher success rate on first attempt vs.
students who received only the recipe.

### Year 8–9: Book Specification Phase

Identified the gap in existing books: no practical guide provided a systematic
diagnosis framework that home bakers could use to understand and correct their own
failures. Designed the book structure around the diagnostic vocabulary.

### Year 10 (current): Draft and Verification Phase

First manuscript draft complete. Currently in QVP verification pass (checking all
technical claims against documented bake log data and bakery-level fermentation
science sources).
